Beef Ularthiyathu

This way of cooking beef is very popular among the Syrian Christian community of Kerala. However, every household has their own Beef Fry recipe or method of preparing Beef Fry.

My mum-in-law shared this recipe with me many years ago ever since this is my go-to recipe without much alteration.

Basically, beef is pressure cooked with spices, until tender. This cooked meat is slow roasted in a pan on low flame until nice and brown. The highlight of this recipe is the Dry Roasted Masala and the addition of fried Coconut Bites.

Ingredients

  • 800gms Beef
  • 2 tbsp Coriander powder
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp Turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tsp pepper powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp ginger finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp garlic finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 25 shallots sliced
  • 1/4 cup Garlic sliced
  • 6 green chilies slit
  • 4-6 sprigs curry leaves
  • Â¼ cup coconut bites
  • salt as required
  • oil as required

Steps

  1. Dry roast the masala: coriander powder, chilli powder, crushed black pepper, turmeric powder and garam masala in a pan
  2. Marinate beef pieces with dry roasted masala mix, ginger, garlic, green chilies, curry leaves, salt and oil.
  3. Add 1 cup water and cook the beef in a pressure cooker.
  4. Pressure cook for about 2 whistles and then remove the cooker from the flame.
  5. Allow it to rest until the pressure goes off then open the cooker.
  6. Cook on a medium flame until the gravy dries out.
  7. Stir the gravy occasionally while cooking, to prevent it from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
  8. Heat oil in a pan; add sliced shallots, garlic, curry leaves and green chilies. Saute until the onions are soft.
  9. Add the coconut bites and fry for few minutes, then the garam masala* and mix well.
  10. Transfer the cooked beef with the masala to the pan and stir fry on a medium low heat until all the moisture disappears and fat separates.
  11. Check for salt and spice, add as needed, you can add ground pepper if needed.
  12. Toss beef on low heat for some more time or until the meat becomes dark in colour.
  13. SERVE HOT with Rice, Porotta, Appam, Naan, or any other bread.
